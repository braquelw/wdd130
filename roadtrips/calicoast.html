Trip Tips
The California Coast is a one-way road trip with some of the best scenery in the U.S. You can start from either end of the state, but I enjoy going from north to south the best. Because this road trip is over 1,100 miles it’s nearly impossible to do everything unless you have at least 2 weeks. Most of the time I recommend doing either the northern coast from Crescent City to San Francisco or the southern coast from Monterey to San Diego. No matter what parts of this trip you choose to see, it will be stunning and awe-inspiring. Whether you want to spend some time in nature or in the big city, you will be able to find your perfect scene along the California Coast!
Crescent City

The most northern town on the California coast. A visit here is not complete without a visit to the Jedidiah Smith Redwoods State Park. Howland Hill Road is a rugged and stunning drive that winds through the towering Redwood Forest, and the Stout Grove Trailhead is an easy walk that gets you a closer look at these magnificent trees. There are some cute things to see and do in town as well, and a favorite stop on our way south is Wilson Creek Beach!

Trees of Mystery

One of the best stops to see the Redwoods up close and personal. You will be educated and entertained as you explore here. They have interpretive trails, a huge Paul Bunyan statue, gift shop, skytrail where you can ride gondolas up the mountain, a restaurant, motel, and canopy trail where you can walk high among the trees!

Newton B. Drury Scenic Parkway

Take this beautiful scenic drive through see miles of the big redwood trees. There are plenty of pull-outs and trails if you want to get out of your vehicle and explore. After head around the grove to Gold Bluff Beach and Fern Canyon. The road to get here is a little rugged, but there is nothing like hiking up the river seeing the beautiful scenery!

Trinidad State Beach

One of our favorite beaches to visit in California, especially at low tide. The views are stunning, and the sea life is abundant! Trinidad Head offers a great hike to the summit, a lighthouse, and stunning viewpoints. The town is also super cute and fun to explore.

Ferndale

Visit this beautiful small-town Victorian village, that is one of my favorite stops along the California coast. Walk through the shops, stop for a meal, and enjoy the sights. You can also get to the Lost Coast from here, which is a natural and undeveloped area for those who like to get off the beaten path.

Avenue of the Giants

Called the finest forest drive in the world, take this stunning 31-mile road to get more amazing views of the beautiful Redwood trees. There are plenty of stops for picnics, camping, river access, shops, and hiking trails. If you want to be inspired, don't miss this drive!

Fort Bragg

A city on the Mendocino coast that is known for its Glass Beach. There are several places to visit nearby, including MacKerricher State Park, Point Cabrillo Lighthouse, and Noyo Harbor. While here make sure to ride the skunk train (or their rail bikes) that weaves its way through the forest.

Point Reyes

A scenic national seashore with a gorgeous lighthouse. This 71,000 acre park boasts a visitor center, hiking trails, expansive beaches, and stunning views. Visit the Morgan Horse Ranch, Kule Loklo, the Tule Elk Reserve, watch for whales, or view the elephant seals during your visit. You can spend a few hours to a whole day at this gorgeous national seashore.

San Francisco

Explore this diverse city and immerse yourself in its rich culture. You will never run out of things to do here, including visiting the Golden Gate Bridge, Fisherman’s Wharf, Alcatraz Island, Museums and Cultural Halls, and beautiful parks. Our favorite way to explore this city is by bike (especially e-bikes), but there are plenty of ways to get around.

Monterey

Visit this beautiful city with their own Fisherman’s Wharf, the Monterey Bay Aquarium, Cannery Row, beautiful coastal views, and the must-do 17-mile drive! You can rent e-bikes, sea cars (that fit 2 people), or charter a boat to do some whale watching. We love to stay in Pacific Grove, and if you enjoy golfing, there is no shortage of courses nearby.

Carmel-by-the-Sea

A cute little town full of personality and things to do. This is the perfect place to spend some time if you’re looking for some luxury. Everything is within walking distance, and you will be able to enjoy hundreds of shops, art galleries, and restaurants. There are few places that have so much to offer all in one location.

Big Sur Coast

One of the most beautiful stretches of coastal scenery includes many charming viewpoints, gorgeous beaches, and great hiking trails. Point Lobos, Bixby Bridge, Pfeiffer beach, Pfeiffer Big Sur State Park, and McWay Falls are must-see places along this beautiful drive. And if you're lucky, you will see an abundance of wildlife!

Morro Bay

See this seaside fishing village that offers a gorgeous setting for all kinds of travellers. Explore the bay around Morro Rock and hunt for sand dollars, spend time on the water kayaking or sailing, and spent time in town shopping, dining, and enjoying the sights. This is the perfect place to spend some time after exploring Big Sur.

Solvang

Visit this charming town with a slice of Danish culture. You will find an array of cute shops, fabulous restaurants, and historic museums. Wander the village, admiring the Danish architecture and ride the historic horse-drawn Solvang Trolley. Even if you don't have much time, this is a must-see stop if you are in the area.

Santa Barbara

A beautiful city that brings together the state's Spanish influence, laid-back vibe, and upscale sensibility. It is housed between vineyards, mountains, and breathtaking beaches, leaving visitors with an abundance of things to do. Visit Shoreline Park, Stearns Wharf, the Santa Barbara Zoo, and even go Sailing during your time in this Mediterranean-style city.

Los Angeles Area

The largest metropolitan area in the U.S. that has hundreds of things to do. Explore Beverly Hills, Long Beach, go to a sporting event or theme park, and get a taste of true city life. No matter what you love to do, you will find it here!

Carlsbad and Oceanside

These cute little coastal towns are great stops if you want to slow down a little and relax.

San Diego

A great location to play on the beach or explore the city and nearly perfect weather. Some of our favorite places here include the Gaslamp Quarter, La Jolla, the San Diego Zoo, Mission Beach, Balboa Park, Old Town, and Sea World. This area also boasts some great mountain hikes, Torrey Pines State Reserve, and the famous 59-mile scenic drive. There is no shortage or things to do in this beautiful city!
